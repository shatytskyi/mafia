## Original Davidoff rules

Dmitry Davidoff created the game in 1986 as a psychology student at Moscow
State University. His original version featured:

- Basic setup: two teams (mafiosi and villagers).
- Player count: «at least 6» players, with 10 recommended for classic play.
- Moderator requirement: a non-participating moderator assigns roles.
- **No special roles**: Davidoff's original game does not include roles with
  special abilities.
- Multiple executions: allowed multiple day-time executions (per day), each
  needing only a plurality.

## Game phases

### Night phase

During night, players close their eyes. The moderator:
- instructs mafiosi to open eyes and identify accomplices;
- has them select a victim through silent gestures;
- processes actions from other roles with special powers.

### Day phase

Players open their eyes and learn who died. They then «discuss and vote to
eliminate one player», with elimination requiring over half the votes.

## Core roles

### Village roles
- **Detective / Seer**: can detect whether a player is a mafioso through
  investigation.
- **Doctor / Protector**: awakens at night after the Mafia have gone back
  to sleep and points at one person to protect.
- **Vigilante**: an innocent who kills every night.

### Mafia roles
- **Standard Mafioso**: participates in night kills.
- **Godfather**: appears innocent despite being the Mafia leader.

### Neutral / killing roles
- **Serial Killer** (a.k.a. Maniac): must kill everyone, innocent or mafia,
  to win.
